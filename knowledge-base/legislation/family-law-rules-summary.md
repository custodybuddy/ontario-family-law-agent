# Family Law Rules - Summary for Ontario Family Law

**Last Updated:** April 19, 2026  
**Jurisdiction:** Ontario  
**Regulation:** O. Reg. 114/99  
**Current Version:** As amended

---

## 📋 Overview

The Family Law Rules govern the **procedure** for family law cases in Ontario courts. They set out:
- How to start a case
- What forms to use
- How to serve documents
- Court processes and timelines
- Case management requirements

**Important:** These are *procedural* rules, not the law itself. They tell you HOW to navigate court, not WHAT the law says.

**Where they apply:**
- Superior Court of Justice (Family Court branch)
- Ontario Court of Justice (family law matters)

---

## 🎯 Key Rules by Topic

### **Rule 1 - General**

**1(3) - Best Interests of Child:**
These rules must be interpreted to secure the best interests of any child affected.

**1(8) - Duty to Primary Dispute Resolution:**
Parties are required to explore dispute resolution options (mediation, arbitration, collaborative law) before or during court process.

**1(8.1) - Exceptions to Mandatory Information Program:**
May be exempt from parenting education if:
- Urgent/emergency case
- Family violence
- Party lives more than 100km from nearest program
- Other prescribed circumstances

---

### **Rule 2 - Interpretation**

**2(3) - Counting Days:**
- Count only business days (exclude weekends and holidays)
- If deadline falls on non-business day, extended to next business day

**Example:** 
If you must respond within 30 days and day 30 is Saturday, deadline is Monday.

---

### **Rule 3 - Time**

**3(1) - Counting Time:**
First day not counted, last day counted.

**3(2) - Extension of Time:**
Court can lengthen or shorten timelines.

**Practical:** Missing a deadline can be serious. If you can't meet deadline, ask court to extend *before* deadline passes.

---

### **Rule 4 - Representation**

**4(1) - Representation by Lawyer:**
Party may represent self or be represented by lawyer.

**4(2) - Disclosure of Representatives:**
Must give name, address, phone number of lawyer (or self if self-represented).

**4(5) - Change of Representative:**
Must notify court and other parties immediately.

---

### **Rule 5 - Where Case Starts and Transfers**

**5(1) - Where to Start Case:**

Generally, in municipality where:
- Applicant lives, OR
- Respondent lives

**For divorce:** Where either spouse resides (or last resided together).

**For custody/access:** Where child ordinarily resides.

**5(7) - Transfer Between Courts:**
Court can transfer case to another court if:
- In interests of justice
- More appropriate jurisdiction
- Convenience of parties

---

### **Rule 6 - Service**

**Types of Service:**

**Personal Service (most strict):**
- Hand-delivering documents to person
- Leaving with adult at residence
- Required for: first application, motions to change final order

**Alternative to Personal Service:**
- Registered mail
- Courier
- Email (if consent)
- Fax (if consent)

**6(2) - Service on Lawyer:**
If party has lawyer, serve lawyer not party (except certain documents).

**6(10) - Substituted Service:**
If personal service not possible, court can order alternative method:
- Post on door
- Email to known address
- Social media message
- Newspaper advertisement

---

### **Rule 7 - Notice of Case, Case Management, Court Dates**

**7(1) - First Court Date:**
Court sets first court date (usually within 6-10 weeks).

**7(2) - Notice to Other Parties:**
All parties must receive notice of court dates.

**7(9) - No Adjournments Without Court Order:**
Cannot postpone court date without court permission.

---

### **Rule 8 - Starting Case**

**8(1) - How to Start:**

**Application:** Most family cases (Form 8, 8A, 8B, 8C, 8D)

**Motion:** To change existing order (Form 15)

**Forms required:**
- Application (Form 8 or specialized version)
- Continuing Record (Form 9)
- Financial Statement (Form 13 or 13.1) if money involved

**8(2) - Claims in Application:**
State specific relief sought (e.g., "sole custody", "child support of $X")

---

### **Rule 9 - Continuing Record**

**9(1) - Contents:**
Every case has Continuing Record with:
- Table of contents
- Application
- Answer
- Reply
- Financial statements
- Court orders
- Other key documents

**9(2) - Format:**
- In folders with metal fasteners (not stapled)
- Numbered pages
- Updated as case proceeds

**Why it matters:** Court reviews Continuing Record for case history. Must be complete and organized.

---

### **Rule 10 - Answering Case**

**10(1) - Deadline:**
Must serve and file Answer within **30 days** of being served with Application.

**10(5) - Answer (Form 10):**
Must respond to each paragraph:
- Admit
- Deny
- Have no knowledge (acts as denial)

**10(6) - Answer AND Application:**
If you want to ask for your own relief, file Answer AND Application in one form.

**Failure to Answer:**
- Respondent in default
- Applicant can proceed without respondent's participation
- Risk of default judgment

---

### **Rule 11 - Reply**

**11(1) - Optional:**
Applicant may serve Reply to Answer within **10 days**.

Usually only necessary if Answer raises new issues.

---

### **Rule 12 - Conferences**

**Types:**

**Case Conference (mandatory):**
- First mandatory step in most cases
- Discuss settlement
- Identify issues
- Schedule next steps
- Required before motion can be heard

**Settlement Conference (often mandatory before trial):**
- Focus on settlement
- More detailed than case conference
- Judge assists with settlement
- Required in most cases before trial

**Trial Management Conference:**
- Final step before trial
- Confirm trial readiness
- Narrow issues
- Estimate trial length

**12(6) - Purposes:**
- Explore settlement
- Narrow issues
- Get orders (consent or interim)
- Schedule next steps
- Case management

---

### **Rule 13 - Financial Disclosure**

**13(1) - Financial Statement Required:**

**When needed:**
- Support claim (child or spousal)
- Property claim
- Exclusive possession claim

**Forms:**
- **Form 13:** Support claims (short form)
- **Form 13.1:** Property AND support claims (long form)

**13(6) - Income Documents:**

Must attach:
- Last 3 years' income tax returns
- Last 3 years' Notices of Assessment
- Recent pay stubs (if employed)
- Business financial statements (if self-employed)
- Corporate tax returns (if shareholder)

**13(11) - Updating:**
Must update financial statement:
- Every 30 days if data changes significantly
- Before any court event where finances relevant
- When requested by other party (reasonable request)

**13(12) - Failure to Disclose:**
Court can:
- Order disclosure
- Draw negative inferences
- Impute income
- Award costs against non-disclosing party
- Strike pleadings (in extreme cases)

---

### **Rule 14 - Motions (General)**

**14(1) - Motion:**
Request for court order before trial.

**Types:**
- Temporary/interim orders (pending trial)
- Procedural orders (extend time, compel disclosure)
- Final orders (simple cases decided by motion)

**14(4) - Notice of Motion (Form 14):**
Must serve at least **4 days** before hearing (or **2 days** if urgent).

**14(10) - Motion Materials:**
- Notice of Motion (Form 14)
- Affidavit (Form 14A) - sworn evidence
- Financial Statement (if relevant)
- Draft order

**14(11) - Responding:**
Respondent may serve:
- Responding materials (at least 2 days before)
- Affidavit in response
- Financial update if relevant

---

### **Rule 15 - Motions to Change Final Order**

**15(1) - Applies When:**
Seeking to change final order for:
- Support
- Custody/access (parenting time/decision-making)

**15(2) - Forms:**
- Motion to Change (Form 15)
- Change Information Form (Form 15A) or Affidavit (Form 15B)

**15(6) - Consent Motion:**
If both parties agree:
- File consent (Form 15C)
- Court usually approves without hearing
- Much faster process

**15(10) - Test for Change:**
Must show **material change in circumstances** (see s.17 Divorce Act or s.37 CLRA).

---

### **Rule 16 - Summary Judgment**

**16(1) - When Available:**
Party can request final decision by motion (no trial) if:
- No genuine issue requiring trial
- Case can be decided on affidavit evidence

**Not commonly used in family law** due to credibility issues, but sometimes appropriate for:
- Enforcement motions
- Calculation issues
- Clear-cut matters

---

### **Rule 17 - Conferences, Case Management**

**17(4) - Case Conference Brief (Form 17A):**
Must file and serve brief at least **4 days** before conference.

**Contents:**
- Background
- Issues in dispute
- Position on each issue
- Settlement proposals

**17(8) - Settlement Conference Brief (Form 17E):**
More detailed than case conference brief.

**17(13) - Conference Judge Cannot Hear Trial:**
Judge at case/settlement conference cannot be trial judge (preserves impartiality).

---

### **Rule 18 - Documents and Evidence at Trial**

**18(1) - Trial Record:**
Applicant must prepare Trial Record with:
- Table of contents
- Pleadings
- Financial statements
- Orders
- Expert reports
- Other key documents

**18(2) - Affidavits:**
Generally, evidence must be given orally at trial (not by affidavit).

**Exceptions:**
- Uncontested evidence
- Procedural matters
- With court permission

---

### **Rule 19 - Costs**

**19(1) - General:**
Court can order one party to pay other party's legal costs.

**19(2) - Considerations:**
- Success/reasonableness of position
- Conduct during case
- Offers to settle
- Financial circumstances

**19(4) - Offers to Settle:**
- Written offer (Form 18)
- If you beat your own offer, may get costs
- Strategic tool

**Typical costs:**
- Case conference: $500-1,500
- Motion: $1,000-3,000  
- Settlement conference: $1,000-2,500
- Trial: $5,000-15,000+

**Self-represented litigants:**
May recover costs for lost wages, expenses (not legal fees since they have none).

---

### **Rule 20 - Appeals**

**20(1) - Deadline:**
Must appeal within **30 days** of order being made.

**20(2) - Notice of Appeal:**
File Notice of Appeal with:
- Transcript of reasons
- Exhibits
- Materials filed at hearing

**Appealable decisions:**
- Final orders
- Some temporary orders (with leave)

**Standard of review:**
- Errors of law: correctness
- Errors of fact: palpable and overriding error
- Discretionary decisions: material error or unreasonable

---

### **Rule 21 - Disclosure in Financial Cases**

**21(1) - Mandatory Financial Disclosure:**

Must disclose:
- Income tax returns (3 years)
- Notices of Assessment (3 years)
- Financial statements
- Bank statements (relevant period)
- Investment statements
- Pension statements
- Corporate documents (if applicable)

**21(2) - Questionnaire:**
Party can serve Financial Statement Questionnaire to request additional documents.

**21(10) - Net Family Property Statement:**
In property cases, must prepare NFP statement showing calculation.

---

### **Rule 22 - Admission of Facts**

**22(1) - Request to Admit:**
Can ask other party to admit facts.

If no response within **20 days**, fact deemed admitted.

**Why useful:** Narrows issues for trial, saves time on uncontested facts.

---

### **Rule 23 - Evidence and Trial**

**23(1) - Affidavit of Service:**
Must prove other party was served (Form 6B).

**23(3) - Expert Evidence:**
Must serve expert's report at least **30 days** before settlement conference.

**23(4) - Trial Procedure:**
- Applicant presents case first
- Respondent presents case
- Each side can cross-examine other's witnesses
- Judge makes decision

---

### **Rule 24 - Costs**

**24(1) - Cost Consequences:**

**Successful party usually gets costs** but court has discretion.

**24(4) - Factors:**
- Reasonableness of positions
- Behavior during case
- Any offers to settle
- Ability to pay
- Public interest/test case

**24(11) - Bad Faith Costs:**
Higher costs if party acted in bad faith:
- Made false allegations
- Unnecessarily prolonged case
- Abused process
- Failed to disclose

---

### **Rule 25 - Orders**

**25(1) - Draft Order:**
Party seeking order must prepare draft order (Form 25).

**25(2) - Formal Order:**
Court issues signed order.

**25(11) - Changing/Setting Aside Order:**
Can ask court to change order if:
- Mistake
- Fraud
- New evidence
- Change in circumstances

## 💡 Practice Tips

**For Self-Represented Litigants:**

**1. Forms Matter:**
- Use correct forms
- Fill out completely
- Sign and date
- File court copy + serve other party

**2. Timelines Are Strict:**
- 30 days to answer
- 4 days for motion notice
- Miss deadline = serious consequences
- Ask for extension *before* deadline

**3. Financial Disclosure Is Mandatory:**
- Complete, accurate, honest
- Update when changes occur
- Attach all required documents
- Failure = court sanctions

**4. Conferences Are Opportunities:**
- Come prepared with proposals
- Be reasonable
- Most cases settle at settlement conference
- Trial is expensive and unpredictable

**5. Keep Continuing Record Updated:**
- Add new documents promptly
- Organize chronologically
- Number pages
- Court relies on it

**6. Document Service:**
- Prove you served other party
- Affidavit of Service (Form 6B)
- Keep copies of everything
- Date and method matter

**7. Get Help:**
- Duty counsel at court (free)
- Law student clinics
- Family law information centers
- Limited scope lawyers (unbundled services)

---

## 🚨 Common Mistakes to Avoid

**❌ Missing deadlines** - Extensions rare after deadline passes
**❌ Not serving documents** - Court won't hear you if other party not served
**❌ Incomplete financial disclosure** - Court will impute income or dismiss claim
**❌ Bringing motion without case conference** - Motion will be adjourned (Rule 14(4.2))
**❌ Not updating Continuing Record** - Court relies on this for case history
**❌ Ignoring procedural requirements** - Can derail entire case
**❌ Not preparing conference brief** - Judge won't hear matter
**❌ Waiting until trial to settle** - Most expensive option
