**Skill Type:** Documentation & Evidence

## 🎯 Description

The Incident Report Generator helps parents create court-ready documentation of concerning incidents, behaviors, or violations of court orders. Reports use neutral, factual language and include all elements required for admissible evidence.

**Purpose:** Transform emotional reactions into factual documentation that judges can use to make decisions about children's safety and wellbeing.

---

## 🔔 Triggers

Activate this skill when the user:

- **Needs to document:** "I need to document this", "how do I record this", "should I write this down"
- **Something concerning happened:** "He did X", "she said Y", "the kids told me Z"
- **Court evidence context:** "evidence for court", "will the judge care about this", "how do I prove"
- **Pattern documentation:** "this keeps happening", "another incident", "same thing again"
- **Safety concerns:** "worried about", "concerned that", "not safe when"
- **Order violations:** "didn't follow the order", "broke the agreement", "violated custody schedule"
- **Incident keywords:** "incident", "report", "document", "evidence", "record"

## 📋 The 18-Value Incident Taxonomy

### CustodyBuddy Standard Categories

All incidents are classified using these standardized types for consistency across the platform:

**1. Communication Violation**
- Excessive messages/calls
- Harassment through communication
- Inappropriate communication content
- Blocking communication about children
- Using children to relay messages

**2. Schedule Violation**
- Late pickup/dropoff (>30 minutes)
- No-show for scheduled time
- Refusal to return child
- Unilateral schedule changes
- Denying parenting time

**3. Safety Concern - Child**
- Unsafe environment
- Inadequate supervision
- Exposure to dangerous situations
- Substance use around children
- Concerning caregivers present

**4. Safety Concern - Parent**
- Threats to parent
- Intimidation or harassment
- Stalking behavior
- Vandalism or property damage
- Threatening messages

**5. Parental Alienation**
- Negative statements about other parent
- Interference with parent-child relationship
- Coaching child's statements
- Withholding information
- Undermining parental authority

**6. Medical Decision Dispute**
- Refusing medical treatment
- Unilateral medical decisions
- Not sharing medical information
- Medication non-compliance
- Emergency medical disputes

**7. Educational Concern**
- Not attending school events
- Educational decision disputes
- Homework not completed during parenting time
- School communication blocked
- Educational neglect

**8. Financial Non-Compliance**
- Late/missing support payments
- Expense reimbursement disputes
- Refusal to share financial information
- Unauthorized expenses
- Dissipation of assets

**9. Substance Abuse Concern**
- Suspected impairment during parenting time
- Child reports substance use
- Observable signs of impairment
- Driving impaired with child
- Substance paraphernalia visible

**10. Mental Health Concern**
- Observable mental health crisis
- Concerning statements to child
- Erratic or unstable behavior
- Self-harm references
- Inability to care for child

**11. False Allegation**
- Unfounded claims made
- Accusations without evidence
- Malicious reporting
- Manipulation of child's statements
- Defamatory statements

**12. Court Order Violation**
- Direct violation of court order
- Contempt of court issues
- Ignoring court-ordered requirements
- Decision-making authority violated
- Restricted activity occurred

**13. Witness Statement**
- Third-party observed incident
- Professional observations
- Child's voluntary statement
- Written statement received
- Documented observations

**14. Police/CAS Involvement**
- Police called to incident
- CAS report made
- Welfare check requested
- Official investigation
- Protective services involved

**15. Domestic Violence**
- Physical violence
- Threats of violence
- Pattern of intimidation
- Violation of protection order
- Abuse in presence of children

**16. Child's Refusal**
- Child refuses transition
- Child expresses fear
- Child shows distress
- Age-appropriate concerns expressed
- Behavioral changes

**17. Positive Interaction**
- Successful co-parenting
- Respectful communication
- Flexible accommodation
- Child's positive report
- Cooperation demonstrated

**18. Other**
- Incidents not fitting other categories
- Multiple category incidents
- Complex situations
- Unusual circumstances
- Pending classification

## 💬 How to Use This Skill

### Step 1: Incident Intake

**Ask clarifying questions:**

```
I'll help you document this incident for court. Let me ask some questions:

1. What date and time did this happen?
2. Where did it happen? (Be specific - address, location)
3. Who was present? (Adults and children - first names only)
4. What exactly happened? (Just the facts, step by step)
5. How did you become aware? (Saw it, child told you, third party, etc.)
6. Were there any witnesses?
7. Do you have any evidence? (Photos, texts, emails, voicemails)
8. Was anyone hurt or upset?
9. Did you contact anyone? (Police, CAS, lawyer)
10. What category does this fit? [Show relevant categories]
```

### Step 2: Structure the Report

**Use this format:**

```
INCIDENT REPORT

Date of Incident: [Date]
Time of Incident: [Specific time or time range]
Location: [Specific address or location]
Incident Type: [Select from 18-value taxonomy]
Reported By: [Your first name only]
Date of Report: [Today's date]

INCIDENT SUMMARY:
[2-3 sentence neutral summary]

DETAILED ACCOUNT:
[Chronological, factual description - see guidelines below]

WITNESSES:
[List any witnesses with their relationship to the situation]

EVIDENCE:
[List any supporting evidence - photos, messages, recordings, documents]

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN:
[What did you do in response? Call police, document, seek medical attention]

CHILD IMPACT:
[Observable impact on child - behavior, statements, physical signs]

SAFETY ASSESSMENT:
[Immediate safety concerns? Ongoing risk? Pattern?]

RELATED INCIDENTS:
[Reference any previous related incidents by date]

NEXT STEPS:
[What needs to happen? Lawyer consultation, police report, CAS call, motion]
```

### Step 3: Apply Court-Ready Language

**Transform emotional language to factual:**

| Emotional | Factual |
|-----------|---------|
| "He was drunk!" | "Observable signs included slurred speech, unsteady gait, and smell of alcohol." |
| "She's crazy!" | "Exhibited erratic behavior including [specific observable actions]." |
| "He's always late!" | "Arrived 45 minutes after scheduled 3:00 PM pickup time." |
| "She screamed at me!" | "Raised voice in front of children, using profanity." |
| "The kids are terrified!" | "Child exhibited signs of distress including crying and refusing to exit vehicle." |

**Guidelines for factual writing:**
- ✅ Specific times, dates, locations
- ✅ Observable behaviors
- ✅ Direct quotes (what was actually said)
- ✅ Witness names and relationships
- ✅ Photos, messages, evidence attached
- ❌ Opinions about intent
- ❌ Assumptions about feelings
- ❌ Character attacks
- ❌ Exaggerations
- ❌ Hearsay without proper framing

### Step 4: Address Admissibility

**Help user understand evidence rules:**

**Admissible:**
- ✅ Your direct observations
- ✅ Your own photographs
- ✅ Messages sent to you
- ✅ Witness statements (with witness willing to testify)
- ✅ Official documents (police reports, medical records)
- ✅ Child's voluntary statement to you (with proper framing)

**Hearsay (requires careful framing):**
- ⚠️ "Someone told me..." (Need that person to testify)
- ⚠️ "I heard that..." (Not admissible unless exception)
- ⚠️ Second-hand information (Document who said it, when, context)

**Never include:**
- ❌ Speculation about what might happen
- ❌ Assumptions about motivations
- ❌ Opinions from non-witnesses
- ❌ Unverified social media posts
- ❌ Anonymous tips

## 📝 Detailed Examples by Category

### Example 1: Schedule Violation

**User says:**
"He didn't show up again! This is the third time this month and the kids were waiting by the window crying. He's doing this on purpose to hurt them!"

**Incident Report:**

```
INCIDENT REPORT

Date of Incident: April 19, 2026
Time of Incident: 5:00 PM (scheduled pickup time)
Location: 123 Main Street, Anytown, ON
Incident Type: Schedule Violation - No Show
Reported By: [Your first name]
Date of Report: April 19, 2026

INCIDENT SUMMARY:
Other parent failed to arrive for scheduled 5:00 PM Friday pickup. Children waited until 6:30 PM before I explained parent would not be coming.

DETAILED ACCOUNT:
Per our parenting schedule, [other parent's first name] was scheduled to pick up [child 1] and [child 2] at 5:00 PM on Friday, April 19, 2026, from my residence at 123 Main Street.

At 4:45 PM, I helped the children pack their bags and they were ready by the front window. At 5:15 PM, I sent a text message: "Are you on your way? Kids are ready." No response was received.

At 6:00 PM, I called [other parent's] cell phone. The call went to voicemail. I left a message: "It's 6 PM, are you coming to get the kids?"

At 6:30 PM, with no communication received, I explained to the children that their [other parent] would not be coming tonight and helped them unpack.

WITNESSES:
Children (ages 7 and 5) were present and observed the wait.

EVIDENCE:
- Text message sent at 5:15 PM (screenshot attached)
- Call log showing call at 6:00 PM
- Voicemail timestamp

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN:
Documented the incident. Provided comfort to children. Maintained normal evening routine.

CHILD IMPACT:
[Child 1, age 7] asked repeatedly "When is [parent] coming?" and appeared confused and disappointed. [Child 2, age 5] cried for approximately 10 minutes after being told [parent] wasn't coming.

SAFETY ASSESSMENT:
No immediate safety concern. Pattern concern: This is the third no-show in the past month (previous incidents on March 15 and April 5, 2026).

RELATED INCIDENTS:
- March 15, 2026: No-show for scheduled pickup
- April 5, 2026: No-show for scheduled pickup

NEXT STEPS:
Document pattern for potential motion regarding reliability of parenting time. Consider makeup time if requested.
```

### Example 2: Safety Concern - Substance Abuse

**User says:**
"The kids said their dad was drinking beer while driving them home and was swerving. I'm terrified! He could have killed them!"

**Incident Report:**

```
INCIDENT REPORT

Date of Incident: April 18, 2026
Time of Incident: Approximately 7:30 PM (per child's account)
Location: Return drive from [other parent's] residence to mine
Incident Type: Safety Concern - Substance Abuse
Reported By: [Your first name]
Date of Report: April 19, 2026

INCIDENT SUMMARY:
[Child, age 9] reported that [other parent] was drinking from a beer can while driving during the return trip on April 18, 2026, and that the vehicle was swerving.

DETAILED ACCOUNT:
On April 19, 2026, at approximately 9:00 AM, [child, age 9] voluntarily stated to me: "Dad was drinking beer in the car last night and the car was going zigzag."

I asked [child] to tell me more without leading questions:

Q: "When did this happen?"
A: "When we were driving home last night."

Q: "What did you see?"
A: "Dad had a can and was drinking from it. It was a blue can like the ones in his fridge that say [beer brand]."

Q: "Where was the can?"
A: "In the cup holder between the front seats."

Q: "What do you mean the car was going zigzag?"
A: "The car kept going toward the yellow line and then Dad would pull it back."

Q: "How many times did this happen?"
A: "I don't know. A bunch of times. I got scared."

Q: "Did you say anything to Dad?"
A: "No, I was scared to."

[Child 2, age 6] was also in the vehicle but when asked separately, said: "I was playing on my iPad and didn't see."

WITNESSES:
[Child, age 9] - passenger in vehicle
[Child 2, age 6] - passenger in vehicle (did not observe)

EVIDENCE:
- Child's voluntary statement (documented above)
- No physical evidence available (did not witness incident)

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN:
1. Documented child's statement immediately
2. Did not coach or lead child
3. Contacted police non-emergency line to report (Case #2026-04-19-1234)
4. Contacted family lawyer for advice (April 19, 2026 at 10:30 AM)
5. Child appeared safe and unharmed

CHILD IMPACT:
[Child] stated feeling "scared" during the drive. No physical injuries observed. Child appears anxious when discussing the incident.

SAFETY ASSESSMENT:
IMMEDIATE SAFETY CONCERN: If accurate, this represents serious safety risk (impaired driving with children). Child should not be transported by [other parent] until this is investigated.

Pattern: No previous incidents of suspected impaired driving documented. Recommend substance abuse assessment.

RELATED INCIDENTS:
None documented.

NEXT STEPS:
1. ✅ Police report filed (Case #2026-04-19-1234)
2. ✅ Lawyer consultation scheduled
3. ⏳ Consider emergency motion for supervised exchanges/transportation
4. ⏳ Request substance abuse assessment
5. ⏳ Monitor for any police follow-up

IMPORTANT NOTE:
This report is based on [child, age 9]'s account. Child was not coached and statement was given voluntarily. I did not witness the incident personally. Police and lawyer have been notified for appropriate action.
```

### Example 3: Parental Alienation

**User says:**
"My daughter told me that her mom keeps saying I abandoned them and that I don't love them anymore. She's turning my kids against me!"

**Incident Report:**

```
INCIDENT REPORT

Date of Incident: April 17, 2026
Time of Incident: During parenting time (evening)
Location: My residence, 456 Oak Street, Anytown, ON
Incident Type: Parental Alienation
Reported By: [Your first name]
Date of Report: April 19, 2026

INCIDENT SUMMARY:
[Child, age 8] reported during parenting time that [other parent] has been making statements about me "abandoning" the family and "not loving them anymore."

DETAILED ACCOUNT:
On April 17, 2026, at approximately 7:00 PM during dinner, [child, age 8] asked me: "Dad, did you abandon us?"

I responded: "No, sweetheart. Why would you ask that?"

[Child] stated: "Mom said that you abandoned us and that you don't love us anymore. She said that's why you left."

I asked: "When did Mom say this?"
[Child] replied: "She says it a lot. She said it yesterday when we were driving to school."

I asked: "How does that make you feel when Mom says that?"
[Child] replied: "I get confused because you seem like you love me when I'm with you."

I responded: "I do love you very much. Your mom and I both love you. We just don't live together anymore. That's between grownups and has nothing to do with you."

[Child] then asked: "So you didn't leave because of me?"
I replied: "No, absolutely not. I will always be your dad and I love you very much."

WITNESSES:
[Child, age 8] present during conversation
No other witnesses to [child]'s statement

EVIDENCE:
- Documented child's statement immediately after conversation
- Previous incident (March 20, 2026) where child asked similar question about being "abandoned"

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN:
1. Reassured child of my love and commitment
2. Did not speak negatively about other parent
3. Documented statement immediately
4. Provided age-appropriate explanation about separation

CHILD IMPACT:
[Child] appeared confused and anxious about parental relationships. Asked reassurance multiple times during evening: "You still love me, right?" Child clung more than usual during bedtime routine.

SAFETY ASSESSMENT:
No immediate physical safety concern. Emotional wellbeing concern: Child is receiving messages that cause confusion about parental love and security.

Pattern: This is the third time in past month child has referenced "abandonment" narrative (previous incidents March 20, April 3, 2026).

RELATED INCIDENTS:
- March 20, 2026: Child asked "Why did you leave us?"
- April 3, 2026: Child stated "Mom said you chose work over us"
- April 17, 2026: Current incident

NEXT STEPS:
1. Consider requesting family counseling for child
2. Document pattern for potential motion
3. Review court order regarding Section 16 obligations (not undermining relationship with other parent)
4. Consult with lawyer about appropriate next steps
5. Continue to reassure child without speaking negatively about other parent

RESPONSE STRATEGY:
Will continue to:
- Reassure child of love and commitment
- Provide age-appropriate explanations
- Avoid speaking negatively about other parent
- Document any further incidents
- Seek professional guidance for child's emotional support
```

## 🚨 Special Incident Types

### When to Call Police FIRST, Document SECOND

**Immediate police (911):**
- Physical violence or threats of immediate violence
- Child in immediate danger
- Weapon present or threatened
- Abduction in progress
- Intruder or break-in

**Document AFTER police response:**
- Get incident/case number
- Get officer names and badge numbers
- Request police report
- Take photos of any damage/injuries
- Note exact time police called and arrived

### When to Call CAS FIRST, Document SECOND

**Immediate CAS (1-800-668-5965):**
- Suspected child abuse
- Child neglect
- Child in unsafe environment
- Concerning injuries on child
- Child reports abuse

**Document AFTER CAS report:**
- Get case number
- Get worker name
- Note date/time of call
- Document child's statement that led to call
- Follow CAS worker instructions

### When Documentation Can Wait

**Safety always comes first. Do not delay emergency response to document.**

Timeline:
1. Ensure immediate safety
2. Contact appropriate authorities (police/CAS)
3. Seek medical attention if needed
4. THEN create incident report
5. Include all emergency response details in report

## ⚖️ Legal Considerations

### Admissibility Rules

**What makes evidence admissible:**
1. **Relevant** - Related to issues in your case
2. **Material** - Could affect court's decision
3. **Reliable** - Properly documented
4. **Authentic** - Can prove it's real
5. **Not hearsay** - Or falls under hearsay exception

### Child's Statements

**Proper framing:**
```
✅ GOOD:
"On [date], [child] voluntarily stated to me: '[exact words]'"
"I asked [child]: '[question]' and [child] replied: '[answer]'"
"[Child] appeared [observable emotion/behavior]"

❌ BAD:
"My child said [other parent] did X" (without context)
"The kids hate going there" (opinion, not fact)
"They're scared of [other parent]" (without observable basis)
```

**Important:**
- Document exact words
- Note if voluntary or in response to question
- Record your questions (prove not leading)
- Describe observable behavior/emotions
- Child's age matters (courts assess reliability)
- Never coach a child's statement

### Pattern Documentation

**Building a pattern:**
```
INCIDENT: April 19, 2026 - Late pickup (45 minutes)
INCIDENT: April 12, 2026 - Late pickup (30 minutes)
INCIDENT: April 5, 2026 - Late pickup (60 minutes)
INCIDENT: March 29, 2026 - Late pickup (40 minutes)

PATTERN ESTABLISHED: 
4 incidents in 3 weeks, average 44 minutes late
Shows unreliability affecting children's schedule
```

**Single incident vs. pattern:**
- Single late pickup = might be excused
- Four late pickups in a month = pattern of unreliability
- Document each incident individually
- Reference pattern in later reports

## ⚠️ What This Skill Will NOT Do

**Boundaries:**

❌ Tell you whether to file a motion (that's legal advice - ask lawyer)
❌ Determine if something is "abuse" (that's for authorities/courts)
❌ Predict how a judge will view it (too many factors)
❌ Tell you whether to call police/CAS (provides guidance only)
❌ Coach you on what to tell your child to say (never appropriate)
❌ Exaggerate incidents to "build a case" (factual only)
❌ Create false documentation (serious legal consequences)

**Always:**
✅ Document only what actually happened
✅ Use neutral, factual language
✅ Be honest about what you witnessed vs. heard
✅ Seek legal advice about next steps
✅ Prioritize child safety over documentation

## 📞 When to Get Help

**Consult a lawyer if:**
- Unsure whether incident warrants court action
- Preparing for upcoming court date
- Incident involves serious safety concern
- Pattern is established and needs legal response
- Court order violations need enforcement

**Call police/CAS if:**
- Child safety is at immediate risk
- Evidence of abuse or neglect
- Threats of violence
- Violation of protection order
- Any emergency situation
