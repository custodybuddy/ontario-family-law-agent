# Ontario Family Law Assistant

**AI-Powered Legal Education for Self-Represented Parents in High-Conflict Custody Situations**

---

## 🎯 What This Is

The Ontario Family Law Assistant is a specialized AI tool that empowers self-represented parents navigating Ontario family law to:

- **Understand their legal rights and obligations** under the Divorce Act, CLRA, and Family Law Act
- **Document incidents properly** for court with admissible evidence
- **Navigate court procedures** and forms with step-by-step guidance
- **Communicate strategically** using de-escalation techniques in high-conflict situations
- **Develop parenting plans** that work in parallel parenting contexts
- **Reduce conflict** and protect children's wellbeing while ensuring their own safety

**Target Users:**
- Self-represented parents facing custody disputes
- Parents in high-conflict or narcissistic abuse situations
- Parents who cannot afford legal representation
- Parents seeking to supplement limited legal help

## 🏛️ Ontario Family Law Overview

### The Big Picture:

**For Married/Divorcing Parents:**
- Use **Divorce Act** (Federal) for parenting arrangements
- Property and support follow **Family Law Act** (Ontario) + Divorce Act

**For Unmarried/Separated Parents:**
- Use **CLRA** (Ontario) for custody and access
- Property division unavailable unless common law (8+ years)
- Support follows same guidelines

**For Everyone:**
- **Child support** determined by Federal Child Support Guidelines
- **Spousal support** determined by Family Law Act
- **Court procedures** follow Family Law Rules (Ontario)

### Best Interests of the Child:

**The paramount consideration** in all parenting decisions. Courts consider:
1. Child's physical, emotional, psychological safety
2. Quality of relationships (both parents, siblings, others)
3. Child's views and preferences (age/maturity dependent)
4. Stability and continuity
5. Ability of each parent to meet child's needs
6. Family violence (if present)
7. Cultural, religious, spiritual upbringing
8. Any other relevant factor

**Maximum contact principle:** Child should have as much time with each parent as serves child's best interests (not necessarily equal time).

### Typical Court Process:

1. **Application filed** (Form 8 or 8A)
2. **Case Conference** (mandatory, usually within 30-60 days)
3. **Settlement Conference** (mandatory before trial)
4. **Trial** (if no agreement)
5. **Order issued** and enforced

## ⚖️ Critical Legal Boundaries

### What This Tool Will Do:

✅ Explain Ontario family law concepts in plain language  
✅ Help you understand your rights and options  
✅ Guide you through court procedures and forms  
✅ Help you document incidents properly  
✅ Teach you strategic communication in high-conflict situations  
✅ Provide trauma-informed, safety-focused guidance  
✅ Direct you to appropriate professional resources

### What This Tool Will NOT Do:

❌ Tell you what to do in your specific case (that's legal advice)  
❌ Predict what a court will decide  
❌ Guarantee any outcome  
❌ Help you coach children's statements  
❌ Support alienation or gatekeeping of children  
❌ Minimize safety concerns  
❌ Replace professional mental health treatment  
❌ Handle criminal law matters

### Red Flag Triggers:

The tool will redirect to appropriate professionals for:
- **Immediate danger** → Call 911 / shelter resources
- **Child abuse/neglect** → Children's Aid Society (1-800-668-5965)
- **Mental health crisis** → Crisis line / emergency services
- **Domestic violence** → Assaulted Women's Helpline (1-866-863-0511)
- **Criminal matters** → Criminal lawyer
- **Complex financial/business** → Accountant / business valuator
- **Custody/support specific advice** → Family lawyer
